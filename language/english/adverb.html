修饰性词类，一般用于修饰动词、形容词或副词。也可修饰名词或句子。【修饰】，可理解为用其他概念从某些方面更具体得表达某个事物。

作用 ​

修饰 ​

  • 修饰名词，形容词或副词时，常位于其前面。

Hardly anyone believed him.
It's quite some time since I wtote to her.

The weather is pleasantly warm.
I am terribly tired.

He falls far behind in his studies.
The engine runs very quietly.

介词短语可充当副词，因而副词可修饰介词短语。

He left shortly after the meeting.

一些常见的搭配：well over my head，well within the time(完全在规定时间内)，right in front of you(就在你前面)，far in the future。

  • 修饰句子时，通常是某些评注性或逻辑连接的词语，如finally，actually等等。需要逗号把它和句子隔开来。

Certainly, I will stand by you forever.
> Therefore, the film is less than perfect. (因而)

  • 修饰动词，副词最常见的是修饰动词，此时其位置较为灵活。

对于表达动作的方式或状态的副词（方式类），一般置于后面。尽可能接近动词，但让位于其他更重要的成分（因为是修饰类）。

He kept quiet resolutely.
They elected him chairman unanimously.

当然，如果副词距离所修饰的动词太远，可见其置于动词前面。

I happily pronounce you man and wife.

多个此类的副词可用 and 或 or 连接。一般遵循【更具体在前】或【更短在前】原则。

He spoke shortly and carefully.

对于强调语气、频率、范围、程度类（限定类）的副词，通常紧跟在行为动词前面，但在助动词、情态动词、be 动词后面。

I badly need a drink. // 语气
You are always late. // 频率
I only heard about the accident yesterday. // 范围
The project is almost finished. // 程度

某些表达【相对时间】类副词，如already，just，still，now等等，也是这种位置放置。

I have just seen the local paper.

副词单词一般放在否定助动词前，always 除外。
He doesn't always leave before 6 o'clock.

对于时间或地点类的副词，通常置于句首或句尾。

Yesterday we went to see a film.

通常的，多个副词的顺序原则是，更具体的在前，不具体的在后。一般表现为：方式动词、地点副词、时间副词。

She sang beautifully in the hall last night.
Come to see me at 3 o'clock in the afternoon.

当然，对于某些经常接地点的不及物动词，如go，live，run等来说，后面的地点副词位于方式副词前。

I ran to school alone because I was late.
They went to school hurriedly after the breakfast.

对于yet，any more，too，as well等词，一般置于句末。

They are not selling it any more.
I haven't read the book yet.

程度副词enough通常在形容词之后。

We are fortunate enough to have a car of our own.

probably 在否定句中可置于助动词或 be 动词之前。

I probably won’t see you. // I **will probably not ** see you.

其他 ​

表达状态 ​

可作为表语或宾语补足语来表达状态。

Time is up. // 时间到了
Everything is over between them.
Let the dog out.

后置定语 ​

通常是时间或地点副词。

The meeting today lasted three hours.
Put the book on the shelf above.

介词宾语 ​

副词可位于介词后面，形成介词短语。有from abroad(从国外)，near here(在附件)，since then(从那时起)，till recently(直到最近)等等。

Since then，robots have joined the fight.

辨析 ​

形式接近，含义不同 ​

close(靠近地) & closely(仔细地)

Sit close to the table, please.
Watch my mouth closely.

free(免费地) & freely(自由的)

we go into the concert free.
I want to wander freely over the hill.

dead(完全地) & deadly(死一般地)

The train stopped dead.
His face is deadly white.

late(晚) & lately(近来)

He arrived late this moring.
What have you been doing lately.

direct(径直) & directly(立刻)

The plane goes dirent to the Beijing.
I hope you will answer me dirently.

most(最) & mostly(大多数地)

You are a most unusual man.
They are mostly from ShangHai.

wide(充分地) & widely(广泛地)

I was wide wake all night.
He was widely known in our community.

high(高) & highly(很)

Does he jump high ?
> Highly recommended location is Little Manly.

deep(深) & deeply(强烈地)

They danced deep into the night.
I was deeply touched by the film.

near(附近) & nearly(几乎)

Spring draws near.
He can play nearly every musical instrument.

含义接近，用法不同 ​

ago：从现在算起的【以前】。 before：从过去或将来算起的【以前】。可泛指【以前做过某事】。

I met her three years ago.
I told you I had met her three years before.

very：通常修饰形容词或副词，如very good，very exciting book。 much：可修饰不可数名词或动词，或比较级。much time，make much of(充分理解)，not thing much of(感觉很一般)。

表达确定性方面，very 要比 much 更强。

too：【也】，用于肯定句，常位于句末，可用有逗号。 either：【也】，用于否定句，只位于句末。

Nice to meet you, too.
I did not either.

enough & too

形容词性用法【太多的】：enough + 复数名词或不可数，too many + 可数复数或too much + 不可数。

I have not enough tools to do this job.
There is too much rain this year.

副词性用法【过度地，太】：adj/adv + enough，too + adj/adv。

She is not old enough to drive a car.
It is too aloud.

so...that：so一般后接形容词或副词。so + adj/adv (+ a/an + noun)，so many/few + nouns，so much/little + noun。

such...that：such一般接名词。such + a/an/adj noun。

He ran so fast that I could not catch up with him.
It was such a bad weather that I had to stay at home.
He is so good a student that we all like him.

so little表示【少的】，而such little表示【小的】。

almost & nearly

意为【几乎】，两者几乎等同。可用于修饰every，all等词。都可用于否定句。

Almost/Nearly all the students went there.

在否定副词，如any，no，nothing，nobody，never等前用almost，不用nearly。

I almost never see her.

nearly前可用程度副词修饰，如very/pretty nearly(几乎)，not nearly(完全不)。

The car is very nearly new.
It is not nearly as cold as yesterday.

fairly & rather

【相当地】。通常前者修饰【好的，令人愉悦的】形容词，后者修饰【坏的，令人感觉不愉快的】形容词。

She is a fairly good singer.
She has a rather poor opinion of your written work.

rather可与too连用或修饰比较级。

These shoes are rather too big.
She drives rather faster than she ought.

英国人用rather修饰自己感兴趣的事物。

I find this subject rather interesting.

quite & rather

修饰名词，意为【十分】。两者几乎等同。

It was quite/rather a shock to see my face on the screen.

若名词前有形容词。

It is a rather bad story.
= It is rather a bad story.

too much & much too

too much中心词为much，意为【太多的】或【太】。作形容词修饰不可数名词，作副词可修饰动词。

You have give me too much (something).
She is too much brothered by the noise.

much too中心词为too，意为【太】。副词性，修饰形容词或副词，但不修饰动词。

The lane is much too narrow for car to pass.

在too many/few前用far而不用much修饰。

We have got far too many opportunities after graduation.

alone & lonely

alone作形容词表【孤生一人的】，指形体的单独。也可用作副词，放在动词后面。

She alone took care of her five children.
After the party he went home alone.

lonely只作为形容词，表精神上的【孤独】或【偏僻的】。

He lived alone in a lonely house, but he didn't feel lonely.

a bit & a little

副词，意为【一点儿，稍微】。

The problem is a bit/little difficult for the little boy.

not a bit意为【一点也不】，not a little意为【完全地】。

He does not mind a bit.
He did not praise her a little. // 非常赞美

表达【一点也不】的短语还可以是not in the least/slightest.

as far as & as long as

as/so far as可表距离，后接名词，意为【远至...】。

He didn't go so/as far as the bridge.

as far as 可用于【就...而言】，as far as possible为【尽可能地】。

As far as I know he will be away for three months.
I will help you as far as possible.

as/so long as意为【只要】，用作连词，引导让步状语从句。

As long as you are happy, it doesn't matter what you do.

as long ago as 意为【早在...】。
